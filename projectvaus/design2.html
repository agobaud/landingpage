Design

02.Design Guidelines

Context based space and surface

We wanted a system that would able to work on any surface in the house, and would recognize the surface that it was on, and adjust accordingly.

Mobility and portability

The system needed to be easily accessed anywhere in the house, and allow for the user to work with it outside of the house as well.

User-based personalization

Because university students usually live with multiple people in one house/apartment, it is important for the system to recognize the different users and adapt to each of their needs.

Keep most of responsibility on user

University students rarely live in the same place for more than a couple of years. We did not want users to become reliant on the system so much that they would forget basic aspects of responsibility. Eventually, they will move into a different house, and we wanted to make sure that they'd still be able to live in a non-smart house.

Tolerance for routine and changes in routine

Many parts of a student's lifestyle are routine, but there are many non-routine tasks that they take part in. The system needs to be able to recognize routine and adapt, so that they system does not become cumbersome to deal with.
