Solution

02.System Features

Ubiquitous System

A common trait the team found among users is that users are often confined to one location when doing certain tasks, such as studying, or waiting for water to boil. To account for the multiple locations the user may be confined to, the team designed a system that would allow users to conveniently access the system from any surface in the house.

Specific Functionality

Users expressed a desire for something to monitor the status of their house in various ways. Our system can inform users of the status of various household elements upon request, including any potential hazards. If a safety hazard arises, or a situation that could soon be problematic, the entire house is warned. Individual users can also monitor the status of items concerning only them, and related warnings will be relayed to that user instead of the entire house.

System Display

The display of our system can be brought up as a projection on both vertical and horizontal surfaces by drawing a square with your index finger. This will show a blueprint of the house next to a list of items currently drawing power. The blueprint will show anything the house considers potentially hazardous via a red icon. Each element of the list can be expanded to show historical time and energy usage patterns of that specific household item. Some items have default statuses that the system is aware of; no notification will sound if your printer is left plugged in. In fact, any notification here will be location, time, and situation unique to ensure that users are notified for either safety hazards or items unique to them. Further explanation of the displays can be found in the next section.
